# Thermodynamics Inside Entropy: Why Most Chemical Structures Do Not Persist

## What is this?
A bridge artifact in the chemistry / foundations lane of the Structural Flow project.

This paper offers a structural reading of thermodynamics. Its central claim is simple:

thermodynamics already operates inside entropy.

Rather than treating entropy as an occasional obstacle to chemistry, the paper treats it as the background condition within which all chemical reactions, rearrangements, and retained structures exist.

## Why should I care?
If you work with chemistry, reaction systems, equilibrium, degradation, or stability, this paper reframes a familiar fact:

most structures formed during interaction do not remain.

That is not a defect in chemistry. It is the normal condition under which chemistry operates.

## What is the claim?
The paper argues that thermodynamics can be read as a persistence-accounting system.

Free energy, equilibrium, activation barriers, kinetics, energy landscapes, boundaries, and cascades all help describe the conditions under which retained chemical structure can resist redistribution long enough to remain.

## What is this not?
This artifact is not:
- a replacement for thermodynamic formalism
- a new physical law
- a mechanism-level chemistry theory
- a claim that persistence is guaranteed or teleological

The quantitative formalism remains essential. The claim here is structural about what that formalism is already accounting for.

## How was this done?
The paper reads standard thermodynamic quantities through a structural lens:
- entropy as the background condition
- persistence as conditional
- boundaries as local protection of retained structure
- cascades as linked continuations that remain thermodynamically constrained
